Being a maker is about bringing ideas to life by harnessing engineering, innovation, art, and science. It’s about belonging to communities that thrive on creativity, sharing, and collaboration—a movement to help others, to give back, and to make a real impact where it couldn’t be made before. It’s the thrill of spending long nights designing, breaking, and rebuilding robots with friends just to earn points in a NASA sand pit. It’s chasing after ideas that are too cool to ignore—projects that are badass, funny, and entirely original. At its core, being a maker is about building things that reflect who I am, while pushing the boundaries of what I can create. Each project is an expression of my curiosity, drive, and creativity. It’s a journey where I challenge myself, connect with others, and leave something meaningful behind.
